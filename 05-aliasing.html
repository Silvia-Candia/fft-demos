5 · Aliasing: why the wall is at N/2

A high-frequency wave and a low-frequency alias pass through the same sample points. From the samples alone they are indistinguishable. (Part 2)

The wall: N samples can only pin down frequencies up to k = N/2 (two samples per cycle, the Nyquist limit). Above it, k and N−k produce identical samples, so for real input the spectrum mirrors about N/2 and the upper half is redundant. Demo 6 shows what complex (I/Q) input changes.
