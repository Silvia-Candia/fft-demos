8A · The butterfly as hardware

The radix-2 butterfly is three hardware blocks: a complex multiplier (the expensive one), an adder, and a subtractor. Click a block to drill in. (Part 1, hardware)

Click the complex multiplier (× W) to see what it costs.

Why the multiplier dominates: adds and subtracts are cheap (a few LUTs). A complex multiply needs real multipliers, which on an FPGA map to DSP slices, a limited resource that drives both area and power. This is why operation counts always count multiplies, and why the whole FFT cost story is really about multiplies.
