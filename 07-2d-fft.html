7 · 2D FFT: rows, then columns, and the corner turn

A 2D FFT is 1D FFTs on the rows, then 1D FFTs on the columns. The hard part in hardware is the transpose between them. (Part 3)

The point: the maths is just 1D FFTs reused. The corner turn (step 3) is a pure memory transpose, and in hardware that memory-bandwidth shuffle, not the butterflies, is the bottleneck. It ties back to the central thread: power lives in data movement.
