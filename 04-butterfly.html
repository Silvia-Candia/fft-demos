4 · The butterfly, and log₂N stages

One butterfly turns two inputs into two outputs with a single twiddle multiply. An N-point FFT is just log₂N stages of them. (Part 1)

direct DFTX[k] = Σn=0..N−1 x[n] · Wkn,  W = e−i 2π/N

FFT butterflyX[k] = E[k] + Wk·O[k],  X[k+N/2] = E[k] − Wk·O[k]

The flow graphs above stay small so they are readable. This readout scales the structure up to the lengths the IP core targets, where drawing every butterfly is hopeless but the counts still follow (N/2)·log₂N.

The structure: the direct DFT connects every input to every output: N·N = N² multiply-adds, a dense mesh. The FFT does the same job in log₂N stages of N/2 butterflies each, so ~ (N/2)·log₂N. That is the whole saving, and it is why the top graph is a hairball while the bottom one stays tidy.
